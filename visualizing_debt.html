< Driven by curiosity about the countries with the highest government debt, I wanted to create this data visualization. The bar chart highlights the top five OECD countries with the highest government debt: Japan, Greece, Italy, Portugal, and the United States. These significant debt levels can hinder economic growth, reduce public spending, and increase financial instability. Consequently, funding for arts organizations may be reduced as governments prioritize debt reduction. The column labels represent the countries, while the row values indicate the average government debt in millions of dollars, and to enhance clarity, the unit of measurement (millions) is specified in the subtitle. The muted blue color scheme maintains a clean and visually appealing aesthetic, reducing visual clutter and improving readability. /p>
